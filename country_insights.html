Understanding the Box Plot

The Box Plot provides a graphical summary of the distribution of a dataset. It shows the following:

  • Minimum (Min): The smallest data point excluding outliers.
  • First Quartile (Q1): The 25th percentile of the data.
  • Median: The middle value of the data.
  • Third Quartile (Q3): The 75th percentile of the data.
  • Maximum (Max): The largest data point excluding outliers.

These five-number summaries provide a concise statistical summary of the data distribution.
